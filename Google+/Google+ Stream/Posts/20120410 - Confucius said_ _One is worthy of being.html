James Gemmell - 2012-04-14 11:12:55+1000
Having done the Java2 SCJP many, many moons ago I'm still a fan. Yes, while modern IDEs will stop you making most mistakes they don't help terribly much when it comes to avoiding (or fixing) deadlocked threads or operator precedence bugs or tell you when to use StringBuffer, StringBuilder or String. They also won't tell you when to use float versus double or BigDecimal or when and how to override hashCode and equals. Yes, all of this stuff is scattered around the various language specs, tutorials and APIs but the SCJP is a convenient excuse to learn it all. Like most qualifications it won't make you a gun coder but it is an indication that you once knew just enough to pass the exam.

As for interview questions, I find that online tech tests give a more objective view of a candidate's capabilities and I have enjoyed every one I've ever done myself. The interview can be then used to explore deeper knowledge of software design and best practice instead of String, hashCode and equals.
