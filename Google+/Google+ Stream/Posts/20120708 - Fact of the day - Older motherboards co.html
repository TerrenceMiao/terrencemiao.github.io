Fact of the day - Older motherboards contain typically 200g per tonne of the precious metal whereas the gold content in newer-generation boards is generally between 30 and 100g per tonne.

This purity is much higher than average gold amount per tonne of ore which is 0.136 oz (3.86g). 

About 320 tonnes gold, value $16 billions, 7500 tonnes silver, worth $5 billions are consumed in all electronic goods produced every year.
