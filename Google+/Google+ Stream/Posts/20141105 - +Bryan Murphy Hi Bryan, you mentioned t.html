Bryan Murphy - 2014-11-06 11:45:46+1100
We use Neo4J because it is a graph database and graph databases are good where relationships between things are important and may need to be queried in both directions. Â For example an SQL database would be good at answering what orders a particular customer has but if you wanted to find out which customers bought a particular item or combination of items it would struggle.

Neo4j is pretty quick at traversing nodes in a graph but we currently use it on a single node and I am not sure about its clustering capabilities.

For more general pros and cons consult google but if you have any more specific questions let me know.
