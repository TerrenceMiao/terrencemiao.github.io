Australia Post's search for relevance

"The blockchain works without third parties, administrators or any kind of key management, Account holders generate their own key pairs and jump into the network without any registration. No one needs to know who they are or what they are. No account holder, no node, no miner and no client needs to be trusted.”

To take Bitcoin as the obvious example: most of its elaborate architecture is that it has to operate without users trusting each other. In an election, trust is necessary – either trust in the one-person-one-vote principle that electoral registration guarantees, or one-person-one-key in an electronic system.
